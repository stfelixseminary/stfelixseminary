Capturing Moments of Faith, Community & Growth

Bishop Elue Blesses New Hostel

In a momentous occasion that marked a significant milestone for St. Felix Seminary, His Excellency Bishop Michael Elue graced the seminary with his presence to officially bless our newly constructed student hostel. This event celebrated not only the completion of an essential facility but also the seminary's commitment to providing comfortable and conducive living spaces for our seminarians. The blessing served as a spiritual affirmation of the seminary's mission and the tireless efforts of all stakeholders who made this construction possible. The new hostel represents hope, progress, and the dedication of our seminary community to fostering an environment where seminarians can thrive both spiritually and academically.

Cultural Day Celebration

Our annual Cultural Day is a vibrant celebration of the rich diversity and heritage of our seminarian community. This spectacular event showcases the various cultures, traditions, and talents of students from different ethnic backgrounds and regions. Through traditional dances, music, culinary delights, and artistic performances, seminarians celebrate their shared faith while honoring their unique cultural identities. Cultural Day serves as a platform for fostering unity in diversity, promoting cross-cultural understanding, and creating lasting memories. It reinforces our seminary's commitment to building a community where every voice is heard, every culture is respected, and every seminarian feels valued and celebrated for who they are.

Inter-House Sports Championship

The Inter-House Sports Championship is an exciting annual event where seminarians compete in various athletic activities while representing their respective houses. This friendly competition promotes physical wellness, team spirit, and healthy rivalry among the seminary community. Beyond the pursuit of medals and trophies, the sports championship embodies the values of discipline, perseverance, and excellence that are central to seminary life. Athletes push their limits, supporting teammates and demonstrating the importance of building a strong body as part of holistic spiritual formation. The event brings the entire seminary community together in a celebration of athleticism, camaraderie, and the joy of friendly competition, creating bonds that extend far beyond the playing field.

Students Investiture 2025

The Students Investiture Ceremony 2025 marked a significant milestone in the academic journey of our seminarians, as new student leaders were ceremonially invested into their positions of responsibility. This solemn and dignified event celebrates the leadership qualities, integrity, and dedication of those chosen to guide their peers through the academic year. The investiture ceremony symbolizes the transfer of trust and responsibility, as newly appointed leaders commit to serving their community with honor and distinction. Wearing their regalia with pride, these students pledge to uphold the seminary's values, support their fellow seminarians, and contribute meaningfully to the vibrant life of our institution. The ceremony reinforces our seminary's tradition of developing not just priests, but outstanding leaders and role models.

Old Boys Celebration (Annual Reunion)

The Annual Old Boys Celebration is a cherished tradition at St. Felix Seminary that brings together alumni and former seminarians to reconnect, reminisce, and celebrate their shared seminary experience. This homecoming event strengthens the bonds of brotherhood that extend far beyond seminary walls, creating opportunities for alumni to mentor current students and share their post-seminary journeys. The celebration is a testament to the lasting impact of St. Felix Seminary on the lives and ministries of its alumni, many of whom have gone on to serve the Church and their communities with distinction. Through fellowship, sharing of experiences, and spiritual renewal, the Old Boys Celebration reinforces the seminary's proud legacy and the enduring spirit of community that defines St. Felix. It serves as a bridge between past and present, inspiring current seminarians with the examples of their predecessors and strengthening the extended seminary family.
